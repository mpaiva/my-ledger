Crypto Terms in Plain English

A quick cheat-sheet, in case any word above was unfamiliar.

🏦 Coinbase

A popular website/app where everyday people buy and sell crypto using regular money. Think of it like an online stock broker, but for coins.

🔐 Ledger wallet

A small physical device (like a USB stick) that holds your crypto offline so hackers can't reach it. "Not your keys, not your coins" — keeping it here means I truly own it.

🔗 The blockchain

A public, tamper-proof record book that everyone can see. Every transfer below is written there forever — which is why anyone can click and verify it.

🧾 Transaction hash

The unique "receipt number" for a transfer on the blockchain. Paste it into a blockchain explorer (or click the button) to look up that exact move.

Note: Amounts and dollar values come straight from my Coinbase and Ledger export files. "Value then" is the price on the day of the transfer; "value today" reflects a later snapshot. Tiny spam/dust tokens were left out to keep things clear. Wallet addresses shown are public and safe to share — they cannot be used to move any funds.
