How to read this ledger (for reviewers new to cryptocurrency)

Cryptocurrency works much like a brokerage account combined with cash transfers. There are two kinds of entries here:

  • Exchange transactions (Buy, Sell, Convert, Reward) happen inside Coinbase — a regulated U.S. company that issues account statements and tax forms, similar to a stock brokerage. These are recorded in Coinbase's books, so they are marked “Internal exchange record.”
  • Wallet transfers (Send / Receive) move coins between Coinbase and the account holder's personal Ledger device (an offline “vault”). These are written to the public blockchain — a permanent, tamper-proof public record — so each one includes a “Verify ↗” link anyone can open to confirm the amount, date, and parties independently.

“In / Out” shows whether the account holder acquired (In) or disposed of / moved out (Out) that asset. Dollar values are the market value at the moment of the transaction. Use the Print / Save as PDF button for a clean copy; printed links include their full web address for reference.
